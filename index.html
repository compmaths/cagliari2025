3 courses in a friendly environment for enjoying mathematics.

Overview

In this program, an intensive 5-day course which will be held at the University of Cagliari on "Theoretical and numerical analysis of differential equations and their applications" at the undergraduate and graduate levels will be offered, prepared by professors from the University of Cagliari and partner universities. This project aims to deepen the study of differential equations, both from a theoretical and numerical perspective, with particular attention to solving problems in scientific and engineering contexts as focusing on the analysis of differential equations applied to real-world models.

Methodology

After the online part, the School will be held physically in the University of Cagliari. It will consist of three courses where theoretical lectures will be given and numerous examples, and exercises will be reviewed. The courses will be complemented with lectures where local and invited professors will stimulate the curiosity of the students, stowing recent research topics related to analysis of differential equations.

Participants are required to attend both online and physical courses.

Credit Recognition

Participants who complete the course will receive 3 ECTS credits. Evaluations for students interested in credit recognition will be based on daily attendance to the school.

Courses

Course 1: General Introduction to Ordinary Differential Equations & Applications to Real-World Models (12h)

Contents:

- Some Types of Linear ODEs and Systems

- Mathematical Modellings and Applications of Linear ODEs and Systems

Speakers: Eylem Öztürk (Hacettepe University) & Fatma Gamze Düzgün (University of Cagliari)

Course 2: Some Types of Linear PDEs and Systems & Numerical Methods for PDEs (6h)

Contents:

- Some Types of Linear PDEs and Systems: Boundary Value Problems and Modelling

- Galerkin Methods for PDEs: Analysis, Implementation and Applications

Speaker: Jose Rafael Rodríguez Galván (University of Cádiz)

Course 3: Mathematical Modelling and Applications of PDEs and Systems (6h)

Contents:

- Introduction to Second Order PDEs

- From Laplace's Equation to Chemotaxis Systems

Speaker: Rafael Díaz Fuentes (University of Cagliari)
